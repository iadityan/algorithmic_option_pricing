# Algorithmic Option Pricing: Bridging Stochastic Calculus and Fourier Transforms

**Organization:** Stamatics Society, IIT Kanpur  
**Duration:** May 2026 – Present (10 Weeks)  
**Mentors:** Utkarsh Sawarn, Aryan Singh, Harsh Vardhan

---

## Overview

This project explores the mathematical foundations of quantitative finance,
culminating in a Python-based European Call Option pricing engine using the
Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) algorithm.

Rather than predicting stock prices, the goal is to **calculate the fair
price of financial derivatives** — the cost of insurance for the financial
system — using the same mathematics used in heat equations and signal processing.

---

## Topics Covered

### Week 1 — Probability & Programming Foundations
- Measure-theoretic probability: σ-algebras, filtrations, probability spaces
- Random variables, independence, conditional expectation
- Martingales and their properties
- Python programming with NumPy, Matplotlib, yFinance

### Week 2 — Derivatives & Financial Markets
- Financial markets: equities, bonds, commodities, currencies
- Derivative contracts: forwards, European call and put options
- No-arbitrage principle and replication
- Intrinsic and time value of options

### Week 3–5 — Stochastic Calculus
- Symmetric random walk, quadratic variation, Brownian motion
- Itô's integral vs. Riemann integral
- Itô's lemma, Itô processes, Itô's formula
- Geometric Brownian Motion as an asset price model

### Week 6–7 — Black-Scholes-Merton Formula
- Dynamic replication and self-financing portfolios
- Derivation of the Black-Scholes PDE from no-arbitrage
- Terminal conditions and closed-form BSM solution
- Put-call parity
- Limitations of the BSM model

### Week 8 — Risk-Neutral Pricing
- Risk-neutral measure and change of measure
- Sub and super hedging strategies
- Re-deriving BSM via risk-neutral pricing
- Real-world limitations

### Week 9–10 — FFT-Based Option Pricing *(Upcoming)*
- Fourier transforms and the FFT algorithm
- Applying FFT to price European Call Options
- Building the pricing engine in Python
